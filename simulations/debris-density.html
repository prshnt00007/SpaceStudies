Debris Density and Kessler Syndrome

Low collision risk

This is a simplified, illustrative model, not a scientific prediction. It is meant to build intuition about Kessler Syndrome: as the density of debris in an orbital shell rises, the chance that a collision creates new fragments (which can then trigger further collisions) increases sharply. Drag the slider to raise the object count, then press "Simulate a cascade event" to see how a single collision can multiply at high densities. Real orbital debris modeling accounts for object size, relative velocity, altitude shell, and much more.
