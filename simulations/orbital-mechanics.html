Kepler's Laws: Orbital Mechanics Visualizer

Distance from focus (r/a)1.00
Relative speed (v/v-circular)1.00

This visualizer shows Kepler's First and Second Laws. The orbit is an ellipse with the central body (the dot in the middle-ish area) at one focus, not the center. Watch how the orbiting body speeds up near the focus (perihelion) and slows down on the far side (aphelion) - that is Kepler's Second Law: a line from the focus to the body sweeps equal areas in equal time. Increasing eccentricity makes the ellipse more stretched and the speed difference more dramatic.
